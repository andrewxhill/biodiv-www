Phylogeny and Phylogeography of Primates

To understand the assembly and evolution of the biota of any region, it is necessary to understand the geological, or physical, evolution of that region. Therefore, the geosciences will play an important role in interpreting the physical evolution of Amazonia to determine how that has affected the evolution of its biota.

It can be argued that the formation of the modern Amazon River system sometime during the last 10 million years was the singular event that triggered the evolution of the modern biodiversity and biogeography of Amazonia. The geosciences will address the questions pertaining to the physical evolution of Amazonia by:

  1. Resolving the question as to when the modern Amazon River system formed by utilizing a variety of dating techniques.
  2. Describing the physical landscapes in existence when the modern river system formed and how they have changed over the past several million years.
  3. Describing if and/or how climates have changed during the course of evolution of the basin and how that might have affected the distribution of plants and animals.
  4. Interpreting fossil flora and fauna for clues as to paleoenvironments and paleobiotas.

The end product of the geoscience research will be a model of the physical evolution of the Amazon Basin over the last 10 million years that will support, and be supported by, models of the evolution of the component parts of its biota.
